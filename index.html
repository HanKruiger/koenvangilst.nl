Koen van Gilst

I'm a freelance German to Dutch technical translator with a Master's degree in Philosophy and over ten years of experience. My clients include Audi, SMA Solar Technology, XING, Deutsche Bahn and many more.

I also like to code and create apps in Javascript. You'll find some examples below. Be sure to also read about my #codeyear as a translator.

Javascripts Labs

  • (2015-16) Word to Line Prices: a very simple web app for translators that converts a price per word to a price per standard line (55 characters).
  • (2015-16) Dutch EU Terminology: a terminology search engine for German-Dutch terminology.
  • (2014) @OverheidEdits: a Twitter bot inspired by @congressedits that monitors and Tweets any edits made to Wikipedia by the Dutch government. It currently has over 800 followers.
  • (2014) Words2Lines: A very simple web app for translators that converts a number of words into lines (and vice versa).
  • (2014) costAnalysis: An app for freelance translators to get an idea of the amount of work involved in a translation (hours) and what you should be charging your clients.
  • (2013)Starfield: I once wrote this program in assembly, but this time I wanted to do it in Javascript. Largely based on this.
  • (2013) Dawkins' Trees: for this web app I've tried to recreate (in part) the program Richard Dawkins describes in his book The Blind Watchmaker.
